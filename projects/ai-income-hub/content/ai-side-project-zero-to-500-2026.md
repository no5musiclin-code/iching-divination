## The Three-Channel Income Model

Here's the framework. You don't need all three channels—start with one, add the second by month two.

### Channel 1: Affiliate Marketing
Promote products you actually use. Earn commissions when people buy through your links.

**Realistic timeline:** First commission in 3-6 months if you publish consistently.

### Channel 2: Digital Products
Sell templates, prompt packs, worksheets, or guides you create once and sell forever.

**Realistic timeline:** First sale in 4-8 weeks if you validate the right product.

### Channel 3: AI-Powered Services
Use AI tools to offer services (content creation, social media management, email writing) at a fraction of the traditional cost.

**Realistic timeline:** First client in 2-4 weeks if you pitch correctly.

## Step-by-Step: Your First 30 Days

### Week 1: Foundation
- [ ] Choose one niche (something you're genuinely interested in)
- [ ] Create your content platform (WordPress.com is free)
- [ ] Write 5 articles targeting search terms people actually Google
- [ ] Apply to 2 affiliate programs (Amazon Associates + one niche program)

### Week 2: Content Machine
- [ ] Publish 4 more articles (aim for 1,500-2,500 words each)
- [ ] Set up Google Analytics on your site
- [ ] Join DEV.to and republish your content there
- [ ] Create your first digital product (start with a simple template or prompt pack)

### Week 3: Traffic & Validation
- [ ] Check which articles are getting the most views
- [ ] Double down on the top-performing topic
- [ ] Share your content in relevant communities (Reddit, Facebook groups)
- [ ] List your digital product on Gumroad (free tier)

### Week 4: First Money
- [ ] Apply to Google AdSense (even if rejected, it pushes you to focus on affiliate)
- [ ] Pitch one potential client for AI-assisted service
- [ ] Track your traffic: Set a goal of 100 views this month
- [ ] Celebrate small wins—they compound

## Common Beginner Mistakes (Don't Do These)

**Mistake #1: Waiting to be an expert**
You don't need to be the best. You need to be one step ahead of your audience. Write about what you're learning as you learn it.

**Mistake #2: Trying to build everything at once**
90% of people start 5 income streams simultaneously and abandon all of them. Start with ONE channel.

**Mistake #3: Ignoring SEO**
Every article you write should be optimized for one specific Google search. Use free tools like AnswerThePublic or Google Autosuggest to find what people are searching.

**Mistake #4: Not publishing consistently**
One article a week beats "perfect" articles published twice a year. Quantity + consistency builds momentum.
